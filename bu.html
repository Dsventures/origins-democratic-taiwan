The origins of democratic Taiwan

Here’s a look at the events that made China turn to Communism. We also explore where the world stands today, witnessing a potential threat of forceful occupation of the self-governed island nation of Taiwan

A secret mission of American operatives formed a partnership with communist army of Mao Zedong. The United States unwittingly played a role in China's conversion to Communism

Mao Zedong Founder of Chinese Communist Party

Chairman Mao Zedong and his Communist Party followers created the People's Republic of China in 1949. Ever since ties between China and West have remained volatile

Jiang Kai-shek Founder of Democratic Taiwan

When World War II broke out the Chinese forces under Jiang Kai-shek received the goodwill and financial support of allies

Japan’s invasion of Manchuria is considered the first act leading to the Second World War. This happened in 1931 - eight years before the war reached Europe

When Japan started invading China, the people wanted both factions – Communists and Nationalists - to fight as one group but Jiang Kai-shek rejected this move. Jiang considered Mao Zedong and his cause the bigger threat

When Japan threatened to occupy northern China, Jiang focused his Nationalist army on the Communists instead. General Zhang Juliang (Marshal of Jiang’s army) was ordered to surround Mao's Stronghold. The Marshal, instead contacted the Communist headquarters in the ancient city of Xi'an

The Marshall placed Jiang under house arrest when he arrived pending a decision by the communist leader on whether to exile or execute him

Jiang kai shek was given little choice in combining forces with Mao's communist army to fight against the invading Japanese

Within 6 months of this new alliance Japanese Invasion began

By end of 1937 Japan considered China a conquered nation. Shanghai, Peking and Canton had all fallen. One corner of Shanghai remained untouched because it had European and United States settlements with the status of colonies called Island Shanghai from 1937 to 1941. These were run by the British, French, Japanese and Chinese in their individual areas

Head of Jiang's intelligence network was an elusive figure of brutal reputation called Dai Li. He headed the department of statistics and investigation. He is believed to have commanded an intelligence force of up to 3 lakh agents

This intelligence force had cracked the Japanese code twice and warned the US of an imminent attack on Pearl Harbour. This was ignored by the US war department. Attack on Pearl Harbour made the US enter the Second World War. USA was now a partner of China and it only recognised the government of Jiang Kai-shek. It supported Nationalist China with weapons and equipment to make sure the Chinese remained at war with Japan

Japan blocked supplies to China from western Pacific Ocean, so the US supplied it through India over the Himalayas in a mission known as ‘Flying the Hump’

China in turn became the listening post for information for Allies. US Naval Intelligence under Milton E Miles formed the Naval Group China with the Nationalist spymaster Dai Li. A special force was trained by this group to achieve several goals including blowing up infrastructure to harass the Japanese and building weather tracking stations in north China and Manchuria. Its techniques ranged from code breaking to use of carrier pigeons for covert long-distance communication.

General William “Wild Bill” Donovan created OSS (forerunner of CIA) in 1941 bringing with him to China subterfuge and ideas that could potentially destabilize its enemies. But Miles and Donovan didn't get along

Donovan in 1943 December sent a discreet message to Mao letting him know that he wanted to meet. But little did he know that Mao's spy had already penetrated OSS. Mao responded to Donovan and welcomed him to be an ally

Despite all the funding and arms Jiang's army failed to make any headway atgainst the Japanese Army in 1944. This was probably because Jiang, instead of using all his resources to fight the Japanese, reserved his best troops for his fight with Mao.

Donovan realized this and let it be known to President Roosevelt that Mao was a better ally than Jiang and that the Communist could fight the Japanese more efficiently. He put together a secret task force that could investigate communist plans, train Chinese operatives and channel significant information back to Washington. This group was called the Dixie Mission

The Dixie Mission consisted mainly of OSS trainers and staff. The rest were members of US foregin service - diplomats, not soldiers, young and fluent in Chinese

Colonel David Barrett arrived at Dixie Mission in June 1944 and discovered a hardened group of guerrillas adept at conducting operations behind enemy lines. The weapons and ammunition meant for Nationalists were now re-routed to the Communists in Yunan

Communists wanted 3 things from the Mission - cash to buy puppets, weapons and ammunition and open military and intelligence cooperation

John Service was born in China, was a missionary kid, had good knowledge of Chinese, not just Mandarin but many dialects. He was known to effectively move through the back alleys of cities and villages and be able to pick up immense amounts of information

John Service suggested changing from the nationalists to the Communists who would be the better for China's future

In late 1944 with the D-day invasion successfully behind them and German armies in retreat, the US began focusing even more on the war with Japan. William Donovan began working with Mao's General Chuday on a daring plan designed to tip the entire balance of the war - a Normandy style invasion of Japanese occupied China on the beaches of Shandong province

Mao and his second-in-command Chou En Lai offered to go to Washington and make a pitch during this time

In November 1944 John Service composed a communication for the Dixie Mission sent by coded telegram to Washington D.C.

In In 1945 Nazi Germany was collapsing under Allied onslaught. On the Pacific American and Allies were slowly recapturing Japan-occupied territory. Power in China was shifting and hence the Dixie Mission suggested in a secret message that the Allies will have to choose a side if both groups (Mao Zedong and Jiang Kai-shek) refused to reconcile

The plan to attack Shandong province was denounced by the Nationalists and Patrick Hurley, the new US Ambassador to China. Due to his interference Dixie Mission was disbanded and sent home in disgrace. Mao lost the support of Americans

In 1945 Japan signed the unconditional surrender and Second World War ended. But the civil war in China resumed

The Americans began supplying the Nationalists with new weapons and flying Nationalist troops to strategic points raising alarm within the Communists

Billions of dollars in US $ were sent to Jiang Kai-shek yet the Nationalist forces collapsed before Mao's armies. Corruption and fraud had bankrupted the entire country and many of Jiang's best troops defected to join the communists

Jiang Kai-shek fled Mainland China in 1949 to join the final remnants of his army on the island of Taiwan

On Oct 1 1949 Mao Zedong declared the birth of Peoples Republic of China. It was now anti-western and anti-American. Within the year China's army fought the American soldiers on the Korean Peninsula. The Dixie Mission was blamed for the loss of China.

Jiang Kai-shek led the nationalist government in Taiwan until his death in 1975. The civil war was still not over with missiles and threats still happening. With roots in the Second World War, it is a stalemate that lasts to this day
